About The Algorithmic Composition Site

Notes for a seminar on Algorithmic Composition held at the Summer School of the Department of Audio and Visual Arts of the Ionian University on June 10, 2017.

Content overview

  1. Introduction
  2. History and Principles: History and principles of computer music languages: Music-N languages, HMSL, interpreted languages, graphical interface environments
  3. Client-Server principle in live sound synthesis and real time algorithmic composition. Syntax and Semantics of Object Oriented Programming environments
  4. Unit genertors in sound synthesis engines. Basic sound synthesis techniques
  5. Writing Synthesis Patches (Synthdefs)
  6. Writing algorithmic generative scores
  7. Live control via MIDI and OSC
  8. Use of Random processes and chaotic generators
  9. Demonstrations of student projects. Feedback

Short Description (150 words)

Algorithmic composition is the composition of music using formalized methods mostly related to mathematics and programming. This course gives a brief historical background of algorithmic composition from early history to the 21st century, and focusses on current techniques. The use of programming for algorithmic composition will be demonstrated, and practical examples will be explored, using the SuperCollider open-source object oriented programming environment. Notions introduced are: Score and instrument, event (note), programming and representing notions from pre-digital music practice (rhythm and tempo, scales and tunings, serialism), randomness and probability, chaos and fractals, markov chains, data based strucures (data sonification), generative grammars, genetic algorithms, neural nets, and sound synthesis techniques (Unit Generators, additive and subtractive synthesis, chaotic generators, spectral modeling, physical modeling, sampling, granular synthesis). Finally, the relationship to other types of algorithmic art forms will be discussed.

Objectives

  1. Provide a historical perspective and overview of current techniques and trends in algorithmic composition.
  2. Get acquainted with some current techniques and tools, as well as related research and its general scientific context.
  3. Learn basic techniques of programming for algorithmic composition.
  4. Experience and explore the musical potential of algorithmic composition with programming in practice.

Duration

4 units of 2 hours each. Total: 8 hours

Required/recommended prior experience for attencance

Participants should have an interest in formal and algorithmic techniques for generating art. Of advantage is a background in music composition, computer programming, mathematics, or constructivist conceptual art and related algorithmic art forms. No background in programming is required, but a readiness to learn the basics of programming is important for successful attendance.

Required Equipment

Instruction Space Infrastructure

  1. Video Data Projector for Laptop.
  2. Sound projection system connected to multichannel mixer. Minimum 2 Channels (stereo), if possible up to 8 channels of sound.
  3. Access to the Internet.

Participants’ Equipment

Participants should bring to class their own laptop computers, (Mac or PC), and their headphones. PC users should use Linux for best results. Obtain Linux MINT XFCE installed on Linux Live USB using the LiLi app.

Course (Participants’) Evaluation Requirements

Present a composition sample or a theoretical paper dealing with algorithmic compositional techniques, and either using programming code or giving a historical treatment. In either case, a paper should be submitted describing the work done, in 2500 words or less.
